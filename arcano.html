Sign Up Page Design for Arcano Partners

Designed an onboarding process for investors
to manage their savings more easily.

Reply time: within 1 hour

Overview

Client

Arcano Partners is an independent firm with more than 19 years of experience in international financial advice and asset management. The company's strategy is focused on maximizing the creation of value for their clients and increasing the positive impact on society and the development of a more sustainable world.

Project Summary

Designing a Sign Up page, usable and with a clean UI, which ensures that potential clients fill in all the information requested, to get a positive ratio of conversions, leading clients to make their first investment.

Problem

Arcano Partners is aimed to help investors and people that want to make the most of their savings.

They lacked a digital onboarding to sign up to their services, which was extremely frustrating for new customers, as they spent more time signing up by phone or in person. Sometimes, this meant a decrease in the number of applications.

The task was to onboard potential clients and investors so that they could set up their accounts on Arcano without any external guidance, in order to increase conversions of new clients.

Goals

The Sign Up page needs to answer a few questions per se on how the onboarding process should look and feel:

  • As a business or company, what information do we need from our potential customers?
  • From a design perspective, how do we style the form fields and the buttons to get a usable interface?
  • What channel do we want to support from a business perspective?

The Sign Up page needs to ensure that the potential client actually fills in all the information requested through the whole process, to get a positive ratio of conversions.

A more specific set of goals would be:

  • Provide potential clients with a simple and understandable digital Sign Up process, with a clean and usable user interface.
  • Increase new user registration, through a painless process, so users can finish the registration process.
  • Lead new users to make their first investment, as a mean to create a good report for stakeholders.

Process

I usually like to use the Double Diamond Theory for the design process over the Desing Thinking approach, as I consider that, with the latter, we make use of the Empathy phase in order to create new products or products that did not exist previously in the market whereas, with the first, the Double Diamond Theory, we focus more on Research and Data in order to Ideate, Develop and Iterate.

For this project, I used an approach based on the Double Diamond Theory, following the steps of Research (discovering information), Synthesis (organising the information), Ideation (generating concepts based on the information discovered), Designing (applying a visual language to the concepts) and Delivering.

Research

Primary Research: General

To better understand the flow of a typical Sign Up process, the first thing I did was a primary research, by reading up on the best practices to design a Sign Up page. This research was general, as I spent some time exploring all kinds of existing examples of Sign Up pages, no matter the business or the company.

I considered this step crucial before benchmarking other similar organizations because this would allow me to know the common painpoints and other design and usability biases.

Secondary Research: Benchmarking

After doing general research, I benchmarked similar companies in the market. As a potential user of every webpage I visited, and according to the best practices on designing a Sign Up page, I could notice a series of painpoints I needed to avoid when designing the onboarding process for Arcano:

Hypotheses: Common Painpoints in Sign Up Pages

  1. Noise & Low Discovery of Value propositions
    2.

    Banners that cover big areas of the screen or they‚Äôre overloaded with text.

  2. Too Many Choices
    3.

    In some cases, there are many CTAs, which may be confusing for a registration process.

  3. Too many questions
    4.

    The registration process is the first screen users interact with. Asking too much information may lead to drop-offs. We should keep the most relevant and necessary information.

  4. Aesthetics
    5.

    The design in some registration processes feel obsolete or don‚Äôt follow design trends. Colors and shapes do not convey a sense of freshness.

  5. There is not a feedback of completed steps
    6.

    Adding a bar of steps is useful for users, as it guides them through the process. This also helps avoiding drop-offs. This complies with Jacob Nielsen's first rule in Usability Heuristic: Visibility of System Status.

Sign Up Screens from the Main Competitors

After benchmarking other companies in the same sector, and according to Jacob‚Äôs Law, the main idea was to create a Sign Up process that felt similar to those from the main competitors, so as to present users with something familiar, but avoiding the painpoints and bad design practices we found.

Jacob‚Äôs Law states that users develop an expectation of products based on their cumulative experience from other products. They way this process works is through mental models, that is, users form a model of how a system works and then they apply that model to new situations where the system is similar (Nielsen, 2000).

Personas

When researching about the ideal User Personas, I fount out that, according to Jim Marous, Co-Publisher of The Financial Brand, host of the Banking Transformed podcast, and Owner/CEO of the Digital Banking Report, when we try to create personas for fintech and digital banking products, we should bear in mind three core dimensions: trust, digital proficiency, and depth of relationship (Marous, 2015). With these three, we can ensure potential customers feel their preferences are heard and understood.

Another thing I found out to be necessary to be taken into accout was users‚Äô behaviour towards fintech products. According to Jea Yu, co-founder of undergroundtrader.com, we can find three types of personas in digital financial services (Yu, 2017):

  • Nomads: These users don‚Äôt stick to traditional models and they‚Äôre willing to try new digital services. 80% of them prefer fully automated banking services.
  • Hunters: These customers want the best quality for the best price and 33% of them prefer to talk to a human advisor when it comes to investment advice.
  • Quality Seekers: In this group, we have the users that don‚Äôt mind paying more in order to obtain more quality. They prefer to have direct phone live assistance and in-person meetings. They usually stay loyal to traditional firms.

We can add some assumptions to the previous information to make our user persona even more specific:

  • All users need to be aged 18 or above 18.
  • All genders.
  • All nationalities.

Alfred

Hunter / Quality Seeker

Alfred works as a lawyer and has a wife and three kids. He often uses a desktop computer or laptop at home, where he daily reads his personal mail before heading his workplace. He often checks his bank accounts balance too. Regularly, he makes purchases and books travels online.

A digital product for investment might be useful for Alfred, as he can be able to have a quick insight on his portfolio evolution everyday in an easy way. However, live assistance and in-person treatment might be preferable in some occasions.

Marina

Hunter / Nomad

Marina is a med student who uses her mobile phone on a daily basis. She‚Äôs curious and always keeps learning new ways to optimise time and resources.

She has some savings from her part-time job and parents and she would like to make the most of them.

Undoubtedly, Marina finds easier managing her savings through a digital product (app / desktop portal), rather than in-person meetings or calls.

Project Takeaways

Some of the things I could notice throughout this project were:

  • Simplicity is a strength: This project is a good example for how simple processes can help users complete tasks more easily and in a more efficient way. A good design is functional, rather than visual. Simple and usable designs can create value for the final user, the company and the stakeholders, since we ensure users complete the desired task.
  • Iteration is key: Something I could notice is how iteration can help with client relationships. Sharing the process with Arcano rather than giving them a final product beforehand helped me hand in the exact product the client was asking for as well as fulfilling the needs of the final users.
