Overview

Context

Company description

Factorial is an all-in-one HR software that allows companies converting never-ending manual processes into integrated and automated ones. It offers a solution for managing company expenses: Factorial Expenses.

Initial background

Factorial released Factorial Cards in April 2022. These allow companies automate their expenses. Customers need to create first an Expenses Wallet account, which provides them with an IBAN, so they can add funds to their wallet and issue new cards.

Problem Solving

Goal What

We needed to raise conversion of Expenses Wallet accounts and Factorial Cards created, as well as to decrease the number of drops when customers started creating an Expenses Wallet account.

Problem Why

From April until August (2022), 5 months after the feature had been released, the amount of customers creating cards was not as initially expected. We needed to spot what was causing customers not to convert, in order to fix it and reach the OKRs established by the team, which ultimately would let us start providing value for customers and stakeholders:

  • Amount of Expenses Wallet accounts created
  • Amount of Factorial Cards created
  • Amount of Factorial Cards active
  • Amount of ‚Ç¨ managed through Factorial Cards

Solution How

For this project, I took the role of Product Design and Product Management. After overviewing the problem, I decided to structure the workflow into a 5-steps process:

  1. Firstly, I did a funnel exploration to have an overview of the problem and generate some hypotheses.
  2. Secondly, I conducted some qualitative research on a small group of customers, to understand better why customers may be dropping.
  3. Thirdly, I debriefed the information from the interviews, to gather common key painpoints and some key learnings which, in turn, helped me validate the initial hypotheses.
  4. Fourthly, I conducted a benchmarking among the main competitors, to see the way they were displaying the information.
  5. Fifthly, I started writing the Product Requirements Definition, and chose a user psychology framework to raoise conversion through design elements. I also defined success metrics measure and track impact, and wrote user stories & Acceptance Criteria, to ensure design quality in every case scenario.
  6. Finally, I started working on the design and all the graphic resources. I also defined the Motion UI elements and interactive animations.

In-depth Review

Generating Hypotheses

We had release the solution of Factorial Cards in April 2022. Since that moment, 5 months had passed and we barely saw conversion. Up to that moment, we had a total of 76 cards created, from 119 accounts.

To have a rough idea about where the problem could be deriving from before we incurred in any development costs, we started to formulate hypotheses, among which we concluded:

  • We're not providing a clear value proposition.
  • We're not offering support through the process.
  • We're not hightlighting the key benefits:
    • Which are the benefits of creating an account.
    • Which are the benefits of creating a card.
  • Customers are missing instructive information:
    • How the account works.
    • How Factorial Cards work.
  • We're not generating engagement through design.

Funnel Exploration

We consulted the funnel to calculate the number of accounts who were dropping in the process and made some discoveries:

  • Some accounts visited the page but did not start the process.
  • Some other accounts visited the page, started the process but dropped.

Generation of Hypotheses

Qualitative research

Once we had formulated the hypotheses, we jumped into intervewing a small group of 10 customers that were using Factorial but had dropped when creating the Expenses Wallet account.

The reason I chose interviews was to approach better the feedback obtained, and revolved around a script that sticked to a structure similar to the following, with open questions to avoid biased answers:

  • Rapport and build
    • We've seen you recently started creating an Expenses Wallet account.
    • Could you describe the process?
  • Peak
    • Did you find any obstacles?
    • Was there any information you were missing?
  • Open feedback
    • Were you to take on the process again, what would you change?
    • From 1 to 5, how would you rate the overall experience being 1 too bad and 5 excelent?

Defriefing

After conducting the interviews we saw that customers answered in similar ways and had some common painpoints, which matched with our initial hypotheses. Among these, we found that nearly every customer had experienced the following pains:

  • Cost: They wanted to know how much the cost was.
  • Transaction fees: They wanted to know implicit fees when using the cards.
  • Key benefits: They wondered what was the ultimate goal for them.
  • Effort: They wanted to know how long it took to create an account.
  • How to: They wanted to know what the process consisted on.

Benchmarking & UX Audit on Competitors

After gathering the common painpoints and defining the key learnings, I conducted a Benchmark and a UX Audit on the closest competitors, according to the business model. The aim of this was to validate what information competitors were chosing to display, why and whether or not this information had any direct relation with the validated hypotheses.

Product Requirements Definition

Once I had gone through the phases of Hypotheses generation, Research, Hypotheses Validation, and Benchmarking, I compiled all this information into the Product Requirements Definition, to communicate the Feature Opportunity with the stakeholders and cross-functional teams to work with: Product Marketing and Engineering.

At this point, together with Product Marketing, we decided to take into account the user physchology ELMR framework, to understand better how customers were making decissions in the funnel and what were their core desires.

ELMR Framework

Through the ELMR Framework we aimed for answering questions like ‚ÄúHow do we improve retention, LTV, virality, or some other variable in our growth model?

Consulting this information in our Reforge program, we understood that users start the decission-making process with emotion, not logic. It's this emotional fuel that drives users towards that action. They don‚Äôt start with logic. They end up using logic to justify and rationalize emotions, but logic does not lead the way. Once again, emotion leads toward the final action

Once the user gets over the decision hill, they will seek a reward because they look to confirm that they made the right decision. A reward increases the chance that they will take that action or decision again on a repeated basis.

This helped me understand better how to carry on with the Product Design phase and how to modularize the elements:

  • In the first place, we displayed a section with the value proposition and a trigger to generate engagement ("Create your Expenses Wallet for free").
  • In the second place, we displayed a section to communicate the key benefits of using Factorial Cards ("Gain time, Set budgets, Pay Safely‚Ä¶").
  • Finally, we decided to display a section with a brief how-to section about what the process looked like in 3 steps.

User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

There were several case scenarios that needed to be taken into account in this initiative. In order to assure quality, I wrote user stories & acceptance criteria following the Gherking methodology:

   
    Given  __________
    When   __________
    And    __________
    Then   __________

This served to ensure that all case scenarios where covered when designing the final solution. In particular, there were two main case scenarios:

  • Customers who did not have an account created, who were based in Spain.
  • Customers who did not have an account created, who were based somewhere other than Spain.

Product Design

Once I had written user stories and spotted all possible case scenarios of the feature, I proceeded to design the landing page, applying the elements in the Design System.

However, there was some bandwith to define new elements that did not exist, such as the graphics for the Physical card and Virtual card (up to that moment, there was no graphical representation of Factorial Cards inside the product).

In this phase, I also explored the interactive animations, using the library Framer Motion, together with the Engineering team. After defining the behaviour of these animations, I jumped and helped adjusting the parameteres of the animation for a optimal interaction. In this step, I got a Gitpod workspace enabled, created a branch and commited the changes after all parameters had been polished.

Success Metrics
